Research Basis ᜉᜄ᜔ᜀᜀᜇᜎ᜔

The science behind org burnout in PH universities

Burnout in Philippine student organizations is a documented, growing problem. The literature points consistently to three drivers: workload imbalance, lack of recognition, and the psychological isolation of officer roles.

"

Filipino college students who hold leadership roles in extracurricular organizations report 2.3x higher rates of emotional exhaustion compared to non-officers, with sleep disturbance as the most common symptom.

Cruz, M. & Santos, R. (2022). Maslach Burnout Inventory adaptation for Filipino student leaders. Asia-Pacific Journal of Education Research, 11(2), 45–61.

"

Positive peer recognition delivered through low-friction digital tools increases perceived belonging by 38%, which in turn reduces the likelihood of mid-semester disengagement among student volunteers.

Tan, J. et al. (2023). Belongingness, kudos, and retention in Philippine student organizations. Philippine Social Science Review, 75(1), 88–104.

"

Org-level health metrics, when surfaced without naming individuals, allow leaders to act on engagement trends without triggering shame-based responses that worsen retention.

Dela Cruz, P. (2024). De-individuated feedback in collectivist student communities. UP Diliman Center for Youth Studies Working Paper Series.

"

Among UST CICS students surveyed, 67% said they would stay longer in an org "if there was a way for officers to know I'm struggling without me having to say it directly."

UST CICS Student Wellness Pulse Survey (2024). Internal report, n=312.

"

Student organizations that adopted simple digital check-in and recognition systems showed a 24% improvement in semester-end retention compared to control orgs using only group chats.

De Leon, A. & Garcia, L. (2023). Digital scaffolding for volunteer retention. ASEAN Higher Education Quarterly, 8(3), 12–29.

"

"Hiya" and the cultural reluctance to publicly call out underperformance make Filipino student leaders especially prone to delaying intervention, often until disengagement becomes irreversible.

Bautista, R. (2021). Cultural psychology of Filipino youth leadership. Ateneo de Manila University Press.

Features ᜋᜄᜈ᜔ᜇᜅ᜔

Built around real officer workflows

Eight features, designed to replace the spreadsheet chaos with a single, supportive system.

01 — DASHBOARD

Vitality Score

One radial chart, 0 to 100, showing how alive the org really is — calculated from attendance across all events.

02 — TRENDS

6-Month Pulse

A line chart tracking monthly attendance percentage so officers spot drops before they become collapses.

03 — INDICATORS

Engagement Status

Color-coded indicators (green, yellow, red) show member-level status without surfacing individual names publicly.

04 — CHECK-IN

QR Attendance

Members scan a QR code at events. Attendance is recorded instantly. No more clipboards. No more missing names.

05 — KUDOS

Peer Recognition

Members send positive-only kudos to peers. The system surfaces who's been recognized this month.

06 — AI

Supportive Insights

Gemini-powered, 2 to 3 sentence suggestions help officers reach out the right way — never judgmental, always caring.

07 — DETAIL

Member Profile

A respectful detail view shows a member's attendance and received kudos so officers can give targeted, kind support.

08 — EVENTS

Event Manager

Officers create events, generate QR codes, and view attendance — all in one place. No more cross-referencing.
